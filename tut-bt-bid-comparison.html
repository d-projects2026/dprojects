How to Use the Bid Comparison Dashboard

Compare contractor bids side by side using weighted scoring, cost breakdowns, and qualification checks.

⏱ 10 min read📊 Beginner

01What is Bid Comparison?

Bid comparison is the systematic process of evaluating multiple contractor proposals against a common set of criteria. In construction procurement — whether for a new residential project in Cairo or an infrastructure initiative across the MENA region — you might receive 5-15 bids for a single package. The D. Projects Bid Comparison Dashboard transforms this overwhelming pile of proposals into a structured, scoreable, and auditable comparison.

02Loading Bids into the Dashboard

1
Open the Template
Launch the Bid & Tender Suite and navigate to the 'Bid Comparison' tab. You'll see columns for up to 10 bidders and rows organized by evaluation category: Commercial, Technical, Experience, Resources, and Program.
2
Enter Bidder Details
For each bidder, fill in the header row: Company name (use coding like Bidder A, Bidder B for objectivity), bid amount, currency, submission date, and bid validity period.
3
Input Commercial Data
Enter each bidder's total price, unit rates for key items, provisional sums, and any qualifications or exclusions. The dashboard auto-calculates percentage differences from the lowest bid.
4
Score Technical Criteria
For each technical criterion (methodology, program, team, equipment), assign a score from 1-10 based on your evaluation of their proposal. The dashboard applies the weighting you've defined to calculate weighted scores.
PRO TIP
Always evaluate technical proposals BEFORE opening commercial envelopes. This prevents price bias from influencing your technical assessment — a fundamental principle in best-value procurement.

03Understanding the Scoring System

The dashboard uses a weighted scoring model. Default weights are: Technical (40%), Commercial (35%), Experience (15%), and Resources (10%). You can adjust these in the 'Settings' tab to match your project's priorities. For complex technical projects, you might weight Technical at 50%. For straightforward works where price competitiveness matters most, Commercial might be 45%. The weighted total score determines the ranking, but the dashboard also flags any bidder who scores below minimum thresholds on individual criteria.

04Reading the Dashboard Outputs

1
Ranking Table
The main output is a ranked list showing each bidder's total weighted score, individual category scores, and price. Color coding highlights the recommended bidder (highest score) in green and any disqualified bidders in red.
2
Price Comparison Chart
A bar chart showing all bids side by side. Significantly low bids are flagged with an orange warning — these may indicate the bidder has misunderstood the scope or is 'buying' the job at an unsustainable price.
3
Compliance Checklist
A pass/fail matrix showing whether each bidder submitted all required documents: bid bond, insurance certificates, company registration, tax clearance, etc. Missing items are highlighted for follow-up.

05Generating the Bid Evaluation Report

Once scoring is complete, the dashboard generates a printable Bid Evaluation Report. This document includes the evaluation methodology, scoring summary, price comparison, recommendation, and any qualifications. This report is your audit trail — it demonstrates that the selection process was fair, transparent, and based on predefined criteria. Keep this on file; it's essential for governance and may be required by clients, auditors, or regulatory bodies.
PRO TIP
Have at least two people independently score the technical proposals, then compare scores. Large discrepancies indicate subjective criteria that need clearer definitions.
